# Relying-Party Assurance Summary

Relying parties need to know whether registry evidence is sufficient for a specific decision. This guide explains how to use the public assurance summary as decision support rather than as an unconditional trust signal.

## Decision pattern

A relying party SHOULD ask four questions before accepting registry-mediated claims:

1. Is the target registry the registry I intend to rely on?
2. Is the assurance evidence still fresh?
3. Does the assurance level match the risk of my decision?
4. Are the limitations acceptable for my use case?

## Safe-use guidance

For AL1 and AL2 decisions, relying parties may use current evidence for low or moderate reliance scenarios if local ecosystem policy allows. For AL3 and AL4 decisions, relying parties SHOULD require independent assessment evidence, incident and change records, and a defined redress path.
