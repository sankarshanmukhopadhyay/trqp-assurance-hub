# Procurement Use Case

## Business problem

Procurement teams often receive narrative claims that a registry service is secure, interoperable, or compliant. Narrative claims are difficult to compare and hard to audit.

## TRQP adoption pattern

A buyer can require suppliers to provide machine-readable CTS conformance evidence, TSPP posture evidence, a Combined Assurance Manifest, and a Public Assurance Summary.

## Copy-ready requirement language

The supplier SHALL provide machine-readable TRQP conformance evidence, TSPP posture evidence, and a Combined Assurance Manifest generated for the evaluated deployment target.

The supplier SHALL disclose the assurance level claimed, generated-at timestamp, validity window, and material limitations or controls not evaluated.

For AL3 or higher deployments, the supplier SHALL provide independent assessment evidence, lifecycle governance evidence, and a redress or contact channel for relying-party concerns.
